# Art Bible

## Visual target

Warm, hand-painted, storybook-fantasy game art. The concept art points toward painterly backgrounds, soft parchment UI, glowing lanterns, cozy gardens, floating clock towers, and toy-like character proportions.

Do **not** copy any specific Braid artwork, characters, level layouts, UI, or composition. Keep the production target to broad, independent qualities: layered parallax, brush texture, warm light, melancholic-but-safe tone, and readable silhouettes.

## Tone words

Cozy, lantern-lit, handmade, gentle, brave, wistful, funny, magical, mossy, golden, restorative.

## Avoid

Blood, graves, realistic body horror, gore, grim death imagery, horror lighting, punishment language, scary skull realism, sharp monster teeth as a dominant visual.

## Bram silhouette

- Oversized skull/head for expression.
- Short child proportions.
- Small backpack.
- Oversized hoodie or jacket.
- Loose shorts/trousers.
- Skateboard has carved runes and warm wheel glows.
- Skeleton form should read like a toy/puppet, not medical anatomy.

## Environment sets

### Rattlewood
Moss, roots, lanterns, cottages, mushrooms, garden paths, owl signs, small wooden bridges.

### Hollow Hills
Caves, mine carts, number stones, warm underground windows, rope ladders, bridges.

### Clocktower Marsh
Frogs, reeds, clock faces, soft fog, brass gears, bell towers.

### Moonlit Market
Coin stalls, kind merchants, hanging lights, exact-change puzzles, memory jars.

## Production asset style

For shipped assets, use separate layers and transparent backgrounds. Background paintings can be large 16:9 plates, but characters, props, UI, coins, particles, and puzzle objects should be exported as isolated PNG sprites or sprite sheets.
