# BRAM: The Almost Boy — Design Brief

## One-sentence pitch

A cozy math adventure where Bram, a boy turned into a skeleton by a strange curse, skateboards, climbs, and solves top-down puzzles to recover the pieces of himself and become human again.

## Design pillars

1. **Restore the boy** — progression is emotional and visible: hands, feet, eyes, voice, heart, human.
2. **Math changes the world** — math is how you land tricks, move bridges, unlock doors, buy memories, set clocks, and help characters.
3. **Cozy-spooky, never scary** — warm lanterns, gardens, owls, mushrooms, soft mystery, slapstick bone crashes.
4. **Game first** — downhill action, platforming, and top-down puzzles must be fun without explaining the curriculum.
5. **Gentle mastery** — wrong answers create hints and funny resets, not shame.

## Core modes

### Rattle Run
Downhill skateboarding. Math choices are placed into the road as answer gates, coin trails, clock ramps, and trick landings.

### Puzzle Platforming
Side-view exploration. Bram jumps, climbs, pushes number blocks, activates levers, and unlocks body abilities.

### Top-Down Adventure Puzzles
Zelda-like rooms, gardens, markets, gates, keys, pressure plates, NPC hints, exact-change puzzles, and clock/logic puzzles.

## First vertical slice

**Rattlewood Roll**

- Player starts as skeleton Bram.
- Learns movement and non-punitive failure.
- Solves addition within 20 during downhill trick gates.
- Solves one platform bridge puzzle.
- Solves one top-down exact-change room.
- Collects 10 Life Sparks.
- Restores Bram's hands.
- Unlocks hand ability: grab rail / pull lever / carry small item.
